Our 2025 CHNA identifies transportation as a primary barrier to care access for LMI patients in our service area — consistent with national evidence that 3–4 million Americans miss or delay medical appointments annually due to lack of transportation.

A county transit authority proposes co-sponsoring an NEMT pilot: $60/round trip, 10% overhead, estimated 50% mitigation of transportation-attributable no-shows. This is both a mission decision and a business decision. The evidence suggests they point in the same direction.

Before adjusting a single input — commit to a prediction. This is not a test. It is the core technique that separates analysts who understand models from analysts who can only operate them.

At default inputs — 40,000 visits, 20% no-show, 30% transport share, 50% mitigation, $225 revenue, $90 cost, $60/trip — what is your predicted first-year net benefit?
Answer: C — approximately $82,800 net benefit, ROI ≈ 1.05×
Transport no-shows = 40,000 × 0.20 × 0.30 = 2,400
Prevented = 2,400 × 0.50 = 1,200 visits
Gross revenue = 1,200 × $225 = $270,000
Marginal cost = 1,200 × $90 = $108,000 | Trip cost = 1,200 × $60 × 1.10 = $79,200
Net benefit = $270,000 − $108,000 − $79,200 = $82,800
The margin of safety: break-even trip cost = $225 − $90 = $135/trip — $75 above the $60 vendor rate.

The VBC Quality Threshold

Value-based contracts structure dollars around quality performance. Kept appointments close quality measures. The earn-back calculation depends entirely on your contract structure — and the difference can be a 4× multiplier on the same quality improvement.

Pattern A — Linear Earn-back (ACO / Withhold)

A dollar pool is withheld and earned back proportionally to quality score across the scoring range. Common in MSSP, ACO REACH.

Earn-back = (Projected − Baseline) / 100 × At-risk pool
Example: 7-pt delta, $500K pool → $35,000
Pattern B — Cliff / Threshold Bonus (Star Ratings)

A bonus is paid only if performance reaches a threshold — binary. Common in Medicare Advantage Star Ratings.

Bonus = Full_bonus IF projected ≥ threshold, ELSE $0
Same 7-pt improvement crossing threshold → $150,000

Module 2 Key Takeaways

📐 The Four-Layer Architecture

FFS base → Coding uplift → CCM/TCM → VBC earn-back. Each layer is additive. Attribution confidence decreases as you move up the stack. Layer 0 (FFS) is the floor; Layer 3 (VBC) requires a formal attribution study.

⚖️ Compliance Architecture

CPT descriptors require AMA licensing. CCM/TCM time must be real, documented, and non-duplicative. Patient travel time is never billable. False Claims Act exposure is not theoretical — it is the primary compliance risk in this program.

📊 Board Presentation Discipline

Anchor to conservative-to-base (FFS + TCM). Present optimistic as Year 2 upside. The ROI on net outlay is accurate but misleading — use program cost ROI as the decision metric. Three scenarios prevent a single-number failure.

🔬 Attribution Methodology

The FFS and TCM attribution chains are high-confidence and traceable. CCM is medium-confidence (enrollment lift attribution required). VBC requires a formal matched-cohort study — the Rochester Epidemiology Project model is the gold standard.
