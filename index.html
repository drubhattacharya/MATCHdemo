Missed Appointments → Chronic Disease Deterioration

In diabetes populations, missed appointments are associated with poorer glycemic control and reduced self-care processes. In hypertension, nonattendance is linked to worse blood pressure control — directly relevant to QPP Measure 236. For high-utilizers, missing post-discharge follow-up increases readmission risk.

Missed HbA1c labs → QPP Measure 001 "not performed" pathway

Missed Appointments → Direct Revenue Loss

Each prevented no-show is a recovered visit worth $225 in net revenue. With 1,200 preventable no-shows per year at a 50% mitigation rate, the gross revenue opportunity is $270,000 annually — before subtracting clinical costs. The marginal clinical cost per recovered visit is $90, leaving a $135/visit contribution margin above program costs.

1,200 prevented × $225 = $270,000 gross/yr

Kept Appointments → VBC Quality Earn-back

Quality programs (ACO REACH, Medicare Advantage Star Ratings) reward performance on measure specifications that require completed clinical encounters. Meridian's ACO REACH contract has $500,000 at risk. A 7-point quality score improvement — plausible with improved appointment adherence — yields $35,000 in incremental linear earn-back, or up to $150,000 under a cliff bonus structure.

7-pt delta × $500K pool = $35,000 linear earn-back

Decision 2: Which CCM/TCM implementation approach is compliant and operationally sound?

Option A

Bill CCM for all enrolled patients every month — the NEMT contact creates the touchpoint we need to justify the code.

Document the ride pickup as the CCM contact event. Count the round-trip duration toward the 20-minute threshold for billing purposes.

Option B

Schedule genuine clinical staff time (care plan review, med reconciliation, symptom monitoring) during NEMT windows. Document staff time separately. Bill only when thresholds are genuinely met.

NEMT creates the reliable contact opportunity. Clinical staff perform and document real care management activities during that window. Time is non-duplicative and threshold-meeting.

Option C

Only bill TCM — avoid CCM entirely because the NEMT contact creates ambiguity about what is billable time.

TCM has clearer face-to-face requirements and is less audit-prone. Skip CCM to eliminate compliance risk entirely.

Option D

Count CCM and TCM time together in the same month when a patient has both — the combined care management is more than 20 minutes total.

During TCM's 30-day period, also bill CCM using the same care coordination time since it all serves the same patient goal.

Module 2 — Key Takeaways

📐 Financial Framework

The ROI model has four additive layers: FFS base → Coding uplift → CCM/TCM → VBC earn-back. Present all four with explicit attribution confidence. Anchor the board recommendation to the conservative-to-base scenario, not the optimistic ceiling.

⚖️ Compliance Architecture

CCM/TCM time must be real, threshold-meeting, and non-duplicative. CPT content requires AMA licensing beyond CMS MLN materials. NEMT creates the contact window; clinical staff fill it with genuine care management work.

🏛️ The Mission Case

For LMI patients managing chronic conditions, a missed appointment compounds over months into worse disease control, avoidable hospitalizations, and deteriorating quality of life. The financial layers are proof that doing the right thing is also financially sound.
